# Christoph Keplinger

**Affiliation:** Max Planck Institute for Intelligent Systems, Stuttgart, Germany; formerly University of Colorado Boulder
**Lab:** Robotic Materials Department

## Research Interests
HASEL (Hydraulically Amplified Self-healing ELectrostatic) actuators, electrohydraulic transducers, artificial muscles, and dielectric elastomer actuators.

## Key Contributions
- Invented HASEL actuators, a new class of high-performance soft artificial muscles
- Demonstrated self-healing electrostatic actuators with muscle-like speed, strength, and efficiency
- Developed Peano-HASEL actuators that linearly contract like biological muscles
- Advanced stretchable ionic conductors for soft transducers

## Selected Publications
- [Science 2018] Hydraulically amplified self-healing electrostatic actuators with muscle-like performance (E. Acome et al.)
- [Science Robotics 2018] Peano-HASEL actuators: Muscle-mimetic, electrohydraulic transducers that linearly contract on activation (N. Kellaris et al.)
- [Advanced Materials 2013] Stretchable, transparent, ionic conductors (C. Keplinger et al.)
