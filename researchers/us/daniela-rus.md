# Daniela Rus

**Affiliation:** Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT), Department of Electrical Engineering and Computer Science
**Lab:** Distributed Robotics Lab, MIT CSAIL

## Research Interests
Soft robotics, programmable matter, autonomous vehicles, distributed robotics, and data-driven design methods for robots. Pioneer in developing soft robots that can locomote, grasp, and manipulate objects using compliant materials.

## Key Contributions
- Co-authored the landmark Nature review "Design, fabrication and control of soft robots" (2015), one of the most-cited papers in the field
- Developed autonomous soft robotic fish and underwater exploration platforms
- Advanced origami-inspired and programmable soft robots
- Pioneered data-driven approaches for soft robot design and control

## Selected Publications
- [Nature 2015] Design, fabrication and control of soft robots (D. Rus, M.T. Tolley)
- [Soft Robotics 2014] Autonomous soft robotic fish capable of escape maneuvers using fluidic elastomer actuators (A.D. Marchese, C.D. Onal, D. Rus)
- [Science Robotics 2018] Exploration of underwater life with an acoustically controlled soft robotic fish (R.K. Katzschmann, J. DelPreto, R. MacCurdy, D. Rus)
- [Autonomous Robots 2020] Model-based control of soft robots (C. Della Santina, C. Duriez, D. Rus)
