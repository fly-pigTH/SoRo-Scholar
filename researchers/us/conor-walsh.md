# Conor J. Walsh

**Affiliation:** Harvard University, John A. Paulson School of Engineering and Applied Sciences; Wyss Institute for Biologically Inspired Engineering
**Lab:** Harvard Biodesign Lab

## Research Interests
Soft wearable robots, exosuits for mobility assistance and rehabilitation, textile-based actuators, and human-robot interaction.

## Key Contributions
- Pioneered soft exosuits -- lightweight, textile-based wearable robots for gait assistance
- Developed soft robotic gloves for hand rehabilitation
- Advanced the soft robotic sleeve for cardiac support
- Bridged soft robotics with clinical applications in rehabilitation medicine

## Selected Publications
- [Science Translational Medicine 2017] A soft robotic sleeve supports heart function (E.T. Roche et al.)
- [Robotics and Autonomous Systems 2015] Soft robotic glove for combined assistance and at-home rehabilitation (P. Polygerinos et al.)
- [Adv. Funct. Mater. 2014] Pneumatic networks for soft robotics that actuate rapidly (B. Mosadegh et al.)
