# Robert J. Wood

**Affiliation:** Harvard University, John A. Paulson School of Engineering and Applied Sciences; Wyss Institute for Biologically Inspired Engineering
**Lab:** Harvard Microrobotics Lab

## Research Interests
Microrobotics, insect-scale flying robots, soft actuators, bio-inspired mechanisms, and novel manufacturing techniques for small-scale robots.

## Key Contributions
- Pioneered insect-scale flying robots (RoboBee) combining rigid and soft components
- Co-developed the entirely soft autonomous robot (Octobot) with integrated 3D-printed fluidic logic
- Advanced multi-material manufacturing techniques for soft and hybrid robots
- Contributed foundational work on soft wearable robots with the Harvard Biodesign Lab

## Selected Publications
- [Nature 2016] An integrated design and fabrication strategy for entirely soft, autonomous robots (M. Wehner et al.)
- [Nature Electronics 2018] Untethered soft robotics (S.I. Rich, R.J. Wood, C. Majidi)
- [Advanced Materials 2014] 3D-printed, externally-and internally-reinforced silicone composites for soft robotics (N.W. Bartlett, M.T. Tolley et al.)
